1. Who we are

[YOUR_LEGAL_NAME_OR_DBA] (“we”, “us”) operates the mobile app Dusk, an ephemeral sunset photo-sharing experience.

2. What the app does

Dusk lets you create or join shared rooms, capture photos during golden-hour windows, and share them with others in those rooms. Shared photos and related content are ephemeral: each item is assigned an expiry time tied to sunset on that calendar day for the location used when you post (not a fixed “24 hours from upload”). After that time, it is no longer visible in the app and is removed from our systems by automated cleanup. Optional features include a map and notifications.

3. Data we collect

  • Device identifier — A stable ID on your device used to participate in rooms and enforce access rules.
  • Account information (optional) — If you sign in with email or Google, we process identifiers and contact information needed for authentication.
  • Content you choose to share — Photos and related data you upload to shared rooms.
  • Location — When you use features that need it (for example capturing or viewing on a map), approximate or precise location may be collected as implemented in the app.
  • Push notification token — If you enable alerts, a token is stored so we can deliver notifications.
  • Preferences on device — Settings such as nickname, onboarding state, and similar items may be stored locally on your device.

4. How we use data

We use this information to run the service: authenticate you if you opt in, sync rooms, deliver shared photos within rooms, show sunset or map context where applicable, send notifications if enabled, and enforce message/photo expiration.

We do not use your data to show third-party advertising in the app, and we do not sell your personal information.

7. Retention

Shared photos and related messages are stored only until their expiry timestamp, which the app sets based on sunset time for that day at the posting location (as obtained from the astronomical time service above). You cannot view them in the app after expiry. We run automated jobs that delete expired records and associated media from storage; there may be a short delay between expiry and complete deletion.
